# Research: North Pakistan Tourism — Demographics & Market (Jul 2026)

> Compiled by research subagent (browser direct-to-source; search engines were
> bot-blocked). Items not verifiable are marked **[NO DATA FOUND]**.

## Methodology note
Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo, Yahoo, Startpage, Mojeek, Ecosia were all blocked by
CAPTCHA/Cloudflare during research. Data gathered by navigating directly to
authoritative sites (Wikivoyage, Airbnb live listings, Wikipedia, operator
sites). **Aggregate 2024–2026 official arrival statistics were NOT found on a
readable public page** — below is the verifiable supply/demand picture instead.

## 2. Price bands (PKR/night, from Wikivoyage + Airbnb)
- Budget guest houses: ~Rs. 150–1,000/night (older Wikivoyage entries; many
  phone-only, no online booking).
- Standard hotels: ~Rs. 3,000–8,000/night (e.g. Naran River Whisper double
  Rs. 4,500; Swat PTDC single/double Rs. 3,200/5,500).
- Premium/resort: Rs. 8,000+/night or US$200+ (Shangrila Resort, Serena Shigar
  Fort Residence "from Rs. 8,000", Serena Swat US$225 avg).

## 3. Air links (verified)
- **Skardu International (KDU)**: ~8–10 flights/day; PIA serves Islamabad,
  Lahore, Multan, Faisalabad, Sialkot, Karachi. Weather-dependent (mountain
  VFR).
- **Gilgit (GIL)**: PIA Islamabad only (42-seat, 45 min, "often delayed by
  several days in winter"); ATR-42/C-130 only.
- **AirSial**: lists Skardu sector (KHI–SKT; likely seasonal/charter).
- **Swat (Saidu Sharif)**: PIA 3× weekly from Islamabad (~30 min).
- "Sehrai Air" **[NO DATA FOUND]** — likely confusion with Serene Air /
  AirSial / K2 Airways.

## 4. Road & jeep/ATV
- KKH: Hunza ~100 km / 2–3 hrs from Gilgit; Islamabad→Hunza up to 24 hrs.
- Skardu road: Islamabad→Skardu 16–20 hrs; Gilgit→Skardu <200 km but ~8 hrs;
  prone to landslides.
- Naran: gateway to Gilgit-Hunza via Babusar Pass (summer); Chilas→Naran 4 hrs.

## 7. Pain points (evidence-based)
1. Weather-dependent flights, multi-day cancellations, no live rebooking.
2. Phone/agent/walk-in dominant for budget stock — no live availability/pay.
3. No centralized digital inventory for North Pakistan (stays + meals +
   transport + activities fragmented).
4. Weak OTA penetration for traditional hotels.
5. Discovery/trust gap for adventure products (paragliding/heli/ATV unverified).
6. Seasonality & road fragility with manual rerouting.
7. Carrier ambiguity (real vs unverified names).
